
(a) Standard: A covered entity may not use or
disclose protected health information, except as
permitted or required by this subpart or by subpart C
of part 160 of this subchapter.
    (1) Permitted uses and disclosures. A covered
entity is permitted to use or disclose protected health
information as follows:
        (i) To the individual;
        (ii) For treatment, payment, or health care
operations, as permitted by and in compliance with
§164.506;
        (iii) Incident to a use or disclosure otherwise
permitted or required by this subpart, provided that
the covered entity has complied with the applicable
requirements of §164.502(b), §164.514(d), and
§164.530(c) with respect to such otherwise permitted
or required use or disclosure;
        (iv) Pursuant to and in compliance with a valid
authorization under §164.508;
        (v) Pursuant to an agreement under, or as
otherwise permitted by, §164.510; and
        (vi) As permitted by and in compliance with this
section, §164.512, or §164.514(e), (f), or (g).
    (2) Required disclosures. A covered entity is
required to disclose protected health information:
        (i) To an individual, when requested under, and
required by §164.524 or §164.528; and
        (ii) When required by the Secretary under subpart
C of part 160 of this subchapter to investigate or
determine the covered entity's compliance with this
subpart.
